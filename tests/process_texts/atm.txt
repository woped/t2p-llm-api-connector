The ATM begins in an idle state, showing a welcome screen and waiting for a customer interaction. When a customer inserts a card or taps for contactless access, the ATM reads the card data and checks whether the card format and network are supported. It then prompts for language selection and requests the PIN. After the customer enters the PIN, the ATM securely sends encrypted verification data to the bank host and waits for a response. If the PIN is incorrect, the machine displays an error and allows a limited number of retries. If the retry limit is exceeded, the ATM ends the session and may retain the card according to security policy.

Once authentication succeeds, the ATM displays available transaction options and receives a cash withdrawal request. The machine asks the customer to choose an account type and enter a withdrawal amount. Before requesting final approval, the ATM validates the amount against local rules, such as denomination constraints and minimum or maximum dispenseable amounts. It then sends an authorization request to the bank host to verify account balance, daily withdrawal limits, and account status.

If authorization is denied, the ATM shows an appropriate message, such as insufficient funds, limit reached, or temporary service unavailability, and allows the customer to retry with a different amount or cancel. If authorization is approved, the ATM initiates the dispense sequence by checking cassette availability, selecting a bill mix, and counting notes internally. During this phase, dispenser sensors monitor note movement to detect jams, double-feeds, or other mechanical anomalies before cash is presented.

After preparation completes, the ATM presents the cash in the output slot and monitors whether it is taken within a configured timeout. If the customer does not take the cash in time, the ATM retracts the notes and triggers a reversal or exception process based on host and bank policy. The machine then offers a receipt option, prints the receipt when requested, and returns the card if card capture is not required. Finally, the ATM closes the session, resets internal state, and returns to the welcome screen for the next user.

Throughout the transaction, the ATM records journal data such as timestamp, terminal identifier, amount requested, amount dispensed, transaction result, and any error codes. It also transmits completion status to the bank host so that account posting, reconciliation, and audit trails remain consistent, including cases where communication timeouts or dispense faults require exception handling.
